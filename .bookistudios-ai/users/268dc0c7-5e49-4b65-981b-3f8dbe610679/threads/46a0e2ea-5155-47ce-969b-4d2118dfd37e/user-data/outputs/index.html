Our Story

From Pike Place to the world

Starbucks was born in 1971, on the cobblestone streets of Seattle's historic Pike Place Market. Founded by three coffee enthusiasts — Jerry Baldwin, Zev Siegl, and Gordon Bowker — our mission was simple: bring the world's finest coffee to the people who love it.

In 1987, Howard Schultz joined and transformed our vision — inspired by the espresso bars of Milan, he created a "third place" between home and work, where community and connection brew as richly as our coffee.

1971
First store opens at Pike Place Market, Seattle — selling whole-bean coffee
1987
Howard Schultz acquires Starbucks and introduces the espresso bar concept
1992
Starbucks goes public — 165 stores across North America
Today
Over 38,000 stores worldwide, serving millions daily
